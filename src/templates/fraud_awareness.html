Anatomy of a Recruiting Scam

Fraudsters are becoming sophisticated. They clone legitimate company websites, use AI to write descriptions, and conduct video interviews. However, they always leave traces.

Asking candidates to purchase a laptop or software from a "vendor" (the scammer) with a promise of reimbursement.

Requesting sensitive Tax IDs, Passport scans, or Bank details "for payroll" before an offer letter is signed.
SCAM EMAIL EXAMPLE

Subject: URGENT: Job Offer - Data Entry Manager ($55/hr)

We are impressed by your profile. We want to hire you immediately without an interview.

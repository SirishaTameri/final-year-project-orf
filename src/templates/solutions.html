Technical Deep Dive

Methodology & Results

Our approach utilizes advanced Transformer-based architectures (BERT & RoBERTa) to detect semantic anomalies in job postings with unprecedented accuracy.

Deep Learning Architecture

Traditional spam detection relies on simple keyword matching. Our system leverages Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers (BERT) to understand the full semantic context of every job listing.

Contextual Embedding

Unlike TF-IDF, BERT converts job descriptions into 768-dimensional dense vectors that capture the meaning behind words, not just their frequency.

Transfer Learning

We fine-tuned pre-trained models on the specialized EMSCAD (Employment Scam Aegean Dataset) to detect domain-specific fraud patterns.

Transformer Pipeline

Input Text ➔ Tokenization ➔ BERT Encoder ➔ Pooling Layer ➔ Classification Head ➔ Fraud Probability

Handling Imbalance (SMOBD)

The Challenge: Real-world data is 95% legitimate. Standard models become biased and ignore rare fraud cases.

To prevent this bias, we implemented the Borderline-SMOTE algorithm tailored for NLP tasks.

Synthetic Generation

Creates new synthetic examples of fraud rather than duplicating old ones.

Boundary Focus

Focuses generation on "hard" examples near the decision boundary.
